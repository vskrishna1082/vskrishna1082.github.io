Personal Webpage of V S Krishna Iyer and a demo ground for some simulations. Best viewed on Firefox desktop.
  • Brazil Nut Effect (Python)
    • When objects of different sizes are mixed and subjected to shaking, the larger objects tend to rise to the top, leaving the smaller ones at the bottom. This produces a density gradient (or, more accurately, a 'packing gradient') - with the lower part of the container densely packed.
  • Phase Separation (C++ and Phyton)
    • The lennard-jones potential contains two tunable parameters - σ and ϵ. σ controls the size of the particles, while ϵ adjusts the depth of the well. Setting up the system such that particles of the same colour attract each other, we observe phase separation. A 'control' system of identical (but differetly) coloured particles is show what happens if the attractive force were absent.
